Qualifying Round

Duration of the Qualifying Round

The qualifying phase will last for a maximum of one month from the start of the tournament. If 75% of the participants have played their third games, then the countdown for the last four days until the end of the qualification begins (they must be completed no later than the date specified above). Therefore, it is not allowed to schedule additional games on days after the announced date. Games that have started or continued on the last allowable day of qualifying games will be played without any additional matches.

General Format of the Qualifying Round

Each participant must play at least three, but no more than five games. For each game, the player receives points: base points for their ranking + additional points (for the difficulty of the nation, for a video recording, and for organizing the event). Points may be deducted for violating the rules of the game. The three best games are selected from the ones played based on the points earned in them. In case of a tie, preference is given to the games that have better additional indicators.Such as nation dificculty etc..

Uniqueness of Opponents in Qualifying Round Games

In each of the first two games, you are required to play against unique opponents, no matter what. Starting from the third game (i.e., in the third, fourth, and fifth games), you may play against two repeated opponents. It is not allowed to play against three or more repeated opponents in one game. You cannot play against the same player more than twice. Having more than two unfinished games at the same time is forbidden.

Time limits for games in the qualification round

If you have not started the next game within a week after the start of the tournament or after the end of your last game, you will be blocked from the tournament. This means that you will no longer be able to play preliminary games, but your results in previous games will not be cancelled, and you may still advance to the playoffs if you have played well up to that point. The week is counted from the host's message with the results of your previous game. If you are unable to play for a week or more for any reason, you must inform the organizers in advance to avoid being blocked from the tournament. For example, if the tournament starts at 5:30 pm on February 18th, you must finish your first game before 5:30 pm on February 25th. If the host publishes the results of your first game at 7:00 pm on February 21st, you must play your second game before 7:00 pm on February 28th. Any game must be completed > within four days from the start of the game. Therefore, if you can only play once or twice a week, participating in the tournament may not be the best idea. If you need to extend the deadline, please notify the organizers in advance.

Additional criteria in case of a tie in points at the end of the qualification round



In case of a tie in points, the following indicators are taken into account in descending order of significance: Number of games played (criterion applies in reverse order) Number of records among all games Number of first, second, third, etc. places among all games Total number of points scored by opponents (15 terms) in the three best games Number of hours at the time of the tournament start (criterion applies in reverse order)
